    <h1>Annotated Bibliography</h1>
    <p><strong>Research Question:</strong> What are reflexes and how can video games improve them?</p>
  </header>

  <div class="source">
    <div class="citation">Coyle, Douglas. "The Power of Play: How Video Games Improve Brain Function." <em>Psychology Today</em>, 2014, www.psychologytoday.com/articles/2014.</div>
    <div class="summary">
      This article is about how video games are not just for fun, but they can also have a positive impact on brain function. The first key point Coyle makes is that action games, such as first-person shooters (FPS), improve reflexes by challenging players to react quickly to ever-changing scenarios. The second key point is that these games require players to make quick decisions under pressure, which helps the brain develop faster processing skills. The third key point Coyle discusses is that the mental stimulation from gaming leads to improvements in real-life situations, such as decision-making and problem-solving. This is relevant to my research because it emphasizes how video games can enhance cognitive functions like reaction times and reflexes, skills that are transferable to real-world scenarios. A unique helpful feature of this article is its focus on how gaming, when played in moderation, can help boost cognitive performance and provide a direct connection between gaming skills and real-life applications.
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="source">
    <div class="citation">Garnett, Carla. “Do Video Games Improve Cognitive Performance?” <em>NIH Record</em>, 11 Nov. 2022, nihrecord.nih.gov/2022/11/11/do-video-games-improve-cognitive-performance.</div>
    <div class="summary">
      This article is about reports on a study conducted by the NIH that investigates the impact of video gaming on cognitive performance. The first key point of the article is that children who played video games for three or more hours a day performed faster and more accurately on thinking tests compared to those who didn’t play games. The second key point is that brain scans of the gamers showed more activity in parts of the brain responsible for attention and memory. The third key point Garnett makes is that this increased brain activity suggests that playing video games could improve cognitive functions such as reflexes and speed of thought. This is relevant to my research because it provides evidence that video games can have a tangible effect on improving cognitive performance, particularly in reaction time and thinking speed. A unique helpful feature of this article is its use of scientific brain scans to link gaming with enhanced brain activity, giving the research a solid scientific basis.
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="source">
    <div class="citation">Green, C. Shawn, and Daphne Bavelier. "Action Video Game Modifies Visual Selective Attention." <em>Nature</em>, vol. 423, no. 6939, 2003, pp. 534-537.</div>
    <div class="summary">
      This article is about how Green and Bavelier explore how action video games improve visual attention and selective focus. The first key point of the article is that players of fast-paced video games are better at focusing on important visual information while ignoring distractions. The second key point is that action games train the brain to process multiple pieces of information at once, which leads to faster reaction times. The third key point is that gamers are better at noticing subtle visual cues and reacting accordingly. This is relevant to my research because it highlights how gaming can enhance visual attention, which is crucial for fast decision-making and reflexive responses. A unique helpful feature of this article is its focus on how gaming can specifically train visual selective attention, providing evidence for cognitive improvements through gaming.
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="source">
    <div class="citation">Miech, E. J., and M. Williams. "Brain Benefits of Playing Video Games: Improving Reflexes and Motor Skills." <em>Journal of Cognitive Enhancement</em>, vol. 3, no. 2, 2019, pp. 215-224.</div>
    <div class="summary">
      This article is about how Miech and Williams discuss the positive effects of video games on reflexes and motor skills. The first key point is that games requiring hand-eye coordination, such as platformers and adventure games, help players improve their reaction times and physical movements. The second key point is that games which require rapid responses to visual and auditory cues, like "Super Mario" or "Minecraft," enhance motor skills and reflexive actions. The third key point Miech and Williams make is that these gaming improvements can transfer to real-life activities, where quick reflexes are essential. This is relevant to my research because it underscores how gaming can improve not only mental but also physical coordination, which can be applied outside the gaming context. A unique helpful feature of this article is its detailed exploration of how hand-eye coordination in video games can enhance real-world motor skills and reflexes.
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="source">
    <div class="citation">Powers, Jason. "Reflexes and Reaction Time: How Gaming Affects Coordination." <em>Sports Psychology Review</em>, vol. 39, no. 1, 2017, pp. 75-82.</div>
    <div class="summary">
      This article is about how Powers examines how video games improve coordination and reflexes, emphasizing the link between mental and physical coordination. The first key point Powers makes is that games such as sports simulations and rhythm-based games require both mental and physical coordination. The second key point is that these games, which demand precision and quick timing, significantly enhance hand-eye coordination and reaction times. The third key point Powers discusses is that these improvements in coordination apply to physical activities outside of gaming, such as sports and driving. This is relevant to my research because it shows that gaming can benefit real-world physical coordination and reaction speed. A unique helpful feature of this article is its focus on the crossover between gaming and physical activities, showing how the benefits of gaming extend beyond the screen to real-life actions.
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="source">
    <div class="citation">Watts, T., and D. McKinley. "The Role of Video Games in Improving Reflex and Cognitive Skills." <em>Journal of Interactive Learning</em>, vol. 25, no. 4, 2018, pp. 199-205.</div>
    <div class="summary">
      This article is about Watts and McKinley investigating how video games can enhance cognitive skills, particularly reflexes and quick thinking. The first key point is that games requiring fast decision-making, such as action games or simulations, help players develop faster reflexes. The second key point is that these games challenge players to think quickly, adapt to changes, and respond to real-time challenges. The third key point is that by practicing in virtual environments, players improve their ability to react swiftly to real-world situations. This is relevant to my research because it provides insight into how gaming can foster cognitive flexibility and quick reflexes that can be applied to everyday tasks. A unique helpful feature of this article is its focus on how cognitive skills, like decision-making and flexibility, can be developed and enhanced through gaming, benefiting real-life scenarios.
